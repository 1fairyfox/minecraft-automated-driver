Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-07-22

This policy describes how Minecraft Automated Driver — the MCP server, the in-game agents, and this documentation site — handles information. It is written to match what the software actually does.

This documentation site

This site requires no account and collects no personal information on a server. Two pieces of shared Fairy Fox state live in your browser's local storage only, and are never transmitted: your reader preferences (theme, accent, text size, spacing, width — the "Aa" menu) and the Fairy Fox coins counter (your coin balance and the record of which pages you've opened today). Both are device-only. You can clear them any time with the reader menu's Reset control, the coins panel's Clear my data link, or by clearing your browser's site data. Coins have no monetary value — see the shared coins explainer.

The driver, on your machine

Minecraft Automated Driver is a local development tool. It runs entirely on the machine you install it on: the MCP server communicates over stdio with the AI client that launched it, and its control channel to the in-game agents binds 127.0.0.1 only. It has no telemetry, makes no network calls of its own beyond the actions you explicitly ask for (such as downloading a Paper server jar from PaperMC's API, or installing dependencies from npm/Maven), and phones nowhere home. Anything it records — logs, screenshots, provisioned test servers — is written to your own disk under the project directories you point it at, and never leaves your machine.

The in-game agents

The optional agents (a Paper plugin and client mods) are inert until explicitly enabled by a launch flag, configuration, or the in-game opt-in button. When enabled, they expose game state only over the loopback, token-authenticated channel above — to software running on the same machine, under your account. They collect nothing and transmit nothing elsewhere.

Third-party services

This site is hosted on GitHub Pages, which may log standard request metadata (such as IP addresses) as a processor. The site's fonts are loaded from Google Fonts (part of the shared Fairy Fox site chrome), which exposes your IP address to Google when pages load — flagged here for transparency.

Analytics & tracking

We use no analytics, tracking pixels, or advertising — on this site or in the software.
